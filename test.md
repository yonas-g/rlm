# Acme Corp — Q4 2025 Financial Report

## Executive Summary

Acme Corp reported total consolidated revenue of $4.82 billion for Q4 2025,
representing a 12.3% year-over-year increase. Operating income reached $1.14
billion, with an operating margin of 23.7%. Net income was $892 million,
or $3.47 per diluted share.

## Revenue Breakdown by Division

### Cloud Services Division
- Revenue: $2.15 billion (up 18.4% YoY)
- Operating income: $645 million
- Key driver: Enterprise migration contracts grew 31% QoQ
- New customers: 1,247 enterprise accounts added
- ARR reached $8.9 billion

### Hardware Division
- Revenue: $1.38 billion (up 4.2% YoY)
- Operating income: $276 million
- Server shipments: 42,000 units (+8% QoQ)
- Average selling price: $32,857 per unit
- Backlog: $2.1 billion (up from $1.7B in Q3)

### Software & Licensing Division
- Revenue: $890 million (up 9.1% YoY)
- Operating income: $312 million
- License renewals: 94.2% retention rate
- New product launches: AcmeDB 4.0, AcmeML Pro
- SaaS conversion rate: 67% of new deals

### Professional Services Division
- Revenue: $400 million (up 6.8% YoY)
- Operating income: $52 million
- Utilization rate: 78.3%
- Average project size: $1.2 million
- Headcount: 3,400 consultants

## Risk Factors

1. **Regulatory compliance**: New EU AI Act requirements may increase
   compliance costs by an estimated $45-60 million annually starting Q2 2026.

2. **Supply chain**: Semiconductor lead times remain elevated at 18-22 weeks.
   The company holds 14 weeks of safety stock for critical components.

3. **Currency exposure**: 40% of revenue is non-USD denominated. A 1%
   strengthening of USD reduces reported revenue by approximately $19 million.

4. **Competition**: Three major cloud providers announced price cuts of 15-20%
   on compute instances. Acme's response strategy involves bundling with
   managed services rather than matching on price.

5. **Talent retention**: Engineering attrition rose to 14.2% (from 11.8% in Q3).
   The company approved a $200 million retention package targeting senior engineers.

## Outlook for Q1 2026

Management guides Q1 2026 revenue of $5.0-5.15 billion, implying 10-13%
YoY growth. Cloud Services is expected to accelerate with the launch of
AcmeCloud 3.0 in February 2026. Hardware margins may face pressure from
component cost increases of 5-8%.

Capital expenditure is planned at $450 million, primarily for data center
expansion in Asia Pacific (2 new facilities in Singapore and Tokyo).

The board approved a $1.5 billion share repurchase program and declared
a quarterly dividend of $0.85 per share, payable March 15, 2026.
